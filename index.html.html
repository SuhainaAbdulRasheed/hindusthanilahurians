Our multi-power electric vehicle integrates EV propulsion, solar assistance, and autonomous intelligence powered by a 4kW PMSM motor. To ensure uninterrupted performance during prolonged steering and braking operations, we engineered an innovative hybrid air-liquid cooling system specifically designed for the autonomous components.

Hybrid Cooling Innovation

Stepper motors in the steering mechanism accumulate thermal stress during sustained position holding, with risks of magnetic degradation beyond 80–100°C. Brake actuators experience additional heat from friction and motor losses, particularly during emergency stops.

Our architecture combines forced air cooling using low-power axial fans and aluminum heat sinks, automatically activated at 60°C through thermistor control. For high load conditions, a shared water-glycol loop derived from the 4kW PMSM motor circulates through a cold-plate system for localized actuator cooling.

ECU-based CAN monitoring prioritizes brake safety and regulates coolant flow, maintaining system temperatures below 70°C. The solution adds less than 2kg weight and remains cost-efficient, while increasing operational reliability by nearly 30% in simulated conditions.

Battery Drive System

High-capacity battery propulsion powers the 4kW PMSM motor, delivering stable torque and controlled acceleration. Integrated thermal management ensures peak efficiency under continuous load.

Solar Energy Integration

Solar panels supplement energy demand, supporting auxiliary electronics and cooling systems, enhancing sustainability and extending driving range.

Autonomous Control

Precision steering and braking are governed through ECU-based control logic. Our hybrid cooling innovation guarantees reliable performance in high-temperature environments without operational compromise.
